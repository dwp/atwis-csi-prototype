A new claim for Carer’s Allowance

We have received a claim for Carer’s Allowance which may affect other benefits

Dear {{ dpName }}

{{ carerName }} has made a claim to Carer’s Allowance on 28 September 2020. They told us they provide you with 35 hours of care or more a week. You do not need to do anything if this is correct.

If {{ carerName }} does not care for you, or provides care for you for less than 35 hours a week, please tell us straightaway. You will need to have your National Insurance number if you call us.

To get Carer’s Allowance a carer must provide 35 hours of care to someone who gets Personal Independence Payment, Disability Living Allowance, Attendance Allowance, Constant Attendance Allowance or Armed Forces Independence Payment or an EU equivalent disability benefit.

Because of this we may look at details of any benefit claims you have made in order to process {{ carerName }}’s claim for Carer’s Allowance. We may also look at details of any benefit claims made on your behalf, for example those made by a partner if you have one.

How this could affect you

Some benefits can be affected if someone gets Carer’s Allowance for looking after you. The benefits that could be affected are set out below.

  • If you get income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance, income-related Employment & Support Allowance, Income Support or Housing Benefit and the amount includes a Severe Disability Premium, you may no longer get that premium
  • If you get Pension Credit and the amount received includes an additional amount for Severe Disability, you may no longer get that additional amount

Communicating in a way that meets your needs

Please tell us if you need us to communicate with you in a different way. For example, large print, braille, British Sign Language, translations, email, audio or something else. Our contact details are at the top of this letter.

For more information you should contact the office dealing with these benefits. If you get a reduction in Council Tax this may also be affected. To find out more please contact your local authority.

{{ carerName }}’s Carer’s Allowance will not affect any Personal Independence Payment, Disability Living Allowance, Attendance Allowance, Constant Attendance Allowance or Armed Forces Independence Payment you may get.

More information

You can find out more about Carer’s Allowance at www.gov.uk/carers-allowance.
